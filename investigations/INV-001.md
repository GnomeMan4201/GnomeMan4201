## Summary

A coordinated inauthentic follow campaign targeting @GnomeMan4201 on DEV.to was identified, reverse engineered, and attributed to a commercial engagement fraud operation — upvote.club / NSBoost — operated by Codemarket OÜ (Tallinn, Estonia). The investigation identified two distinct threat vectors: an automated bot farm (2,585 fake accounts) and a commercial engagement farm (981 real users purchasing follows). Full operator attribution was achieved including legal entity, named individuals, infrastructure map, and product architecture. Findings were disclosed to DEV.to Trust & Safety and GitHub Security. A post-publication infrastructure migration by the operator was detected within hours via automated monitoring.

## Methodology

### Follower collection
DEV.to public API (`/api/followers/users`) paginated at 1000/page. Collected username, follower/following counts, article count, comment count, profile image URL, join date, bio for all 3,566 followers.

### Heuristic scoring
Five signals (1 point each): zero_followers, zero_articles, no_comments, empty_bio, hash_username. Score ≥ 3 = flagged. Score 5 = bot. Tier 4 (real engagement farm users with bios) separated from bot tiers.

### Avatar fingerprinting
MD5 clustering (exact duplicates) and perceptual hash clustering (visually similar). Groups of 3+ sharing exact hash = coordinated fake account creation.

### S3 gap analysis
DEV.to profile images stored in S3 with sequential numeric IDs. IDs serve as monotonic registration proxies. Gap analysis within image clusters reveals same-session batch registrations.

### Density anomaly
81.5% of flagged accounts (2,907 of 3,566) had S3 IDs in range 3,900,000–3,951,600 — a 63x density anomaly vs background registration rates.

### Extension reverse engineering
Chrome extension `fkiaohmeeoiipoknngcppjbkinaamnof` (Helper App v1.1.26) decompiled. HMAC-SHA256 request signing documented. Three task delivery paths: URL parameter interception, dev.to `externally_connectable` side channel, MAIN-world GraphQL hooks.
